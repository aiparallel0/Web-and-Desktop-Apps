Getting Started with Receipt Extractor

Welcome to Receipt Extractor! Follow these simple steps to extract data from your receipts in seconds.

1

Upload Your Receipt

Drag and drop your receipt image onto the upload area, click to browse files, or use your device camera. We support JPEG, PNG, WebP, PDF, and HEIC formats up to 100MB.

2

Choose an OCR Model (Optional)

For most receipts, our auto-selection works great. For specific needs, choose from Tesseract (fast), EasyOCR (accurate), PaddleOCR (multilingual), or Florence-2 AI (complex layouts).

3

Review Extracted Data

Our AI extracts merchant name, date, items, prices, tax, and total. Review the results side-by-side with your original receipt image.

4

Export Your Data

Download your extracted data in JSON, CSV, or TXT format. Pro users can also export to Excel and PDF reports.

Pro Tips for Best Results

  • Ensure good lighting and focus when photographing receipts
  • Include the entire receipt in the image without cropping
  • For thermal receipts, capture them before they fade
  • Use Florence-2 AI for complex or unusual receipt layouts

Batch Processing Guide

Process multiple receipts at once to save time. Batch processing is available on Pro and Business plans.

1

Select Multiple Files

Click the batch upload button or drag multiple files at once. You can upload up to 50 receipts per batch (up to 100 on Business plans).

2

Configure Processing Options

Choose your preferred OCR model or use "Auto" for optimal model selection per image. Select output format (JSON, CSV, or Excel).

3

Monitor Progress

Track real-time progress as each receipt is processed. Failed extractions are flagged for review.

4

Download Results

Download all results in a single ZIP file containing individual extraction files plus a summary spreadsheet.

API Batch Processing

For automated workflows, use our batch API endpoint:

POST /api/extract/batch-multi

# Request (multipart/form-data)
images: [file1.jpg, file2.png, file3.pdf]
model_id: "auto" # or specific model
use_all_models: false

# Response
{
"success": true,
"images_count": 3,
"results": [...]
}

Batch Processing Tips

  • Name files descriptively for easier result matching
  • Group similar receipt types for consistent model performance
  • Use "use_all_models: true" to compare OCR engines
  • Schedule large batches during off-peak hours for faster processing

Model Finetuning

Improve OCR accuracy for your specific receipt types by finetuning our AI models with your own training data. Available on Business and Enterprise plans.

Why Finetune?

  • Custom Receipt Formats: Train the model to recognize your unique receipt layouts
  • Specialized Fields: Improve extraction of industry-specific data points
  • Higher Accuracy: Achieve 99%+ accuracy on your specific receipt types
  • Faster Processing: Optimized models process your receipts more efficiently

Finetuning Process

1

Prepare Training Data

Collect 50-500 labeled receipt images. Include ground truth JSON with merchant name, date, items, and totals for each receipt.

2

Create Finetuning Job

Use the API or dashboard to upload training data and configure training parameters (epochs, learning rate, batch size).

3

Train the Model

Training runs on our cloud infrastructure (HuggingFace, Replicate, or RunPod). Monitor progress and view training metrics.

4

Deploy & Use

Once complete, your finetuned model appears in your model list. Use it for extraction or download for on-premise deployment.

You can upload a receipt in three ways:

  1. Drag & Drop: Simply drag your receipt image onto the upload area on the homepage.
  2. Browse Files: Click the "Browse Files" button to select a file from your computer.
  3. Take Photo: On mobile devices, use the "Take Photo" option to capture a receipt with your camera.

We support the following image formats:

  • JPEG/JPG
  • PNG
  • WebP
  • BMP
  • TIFF
  • PDF (single page)
  • HEIC/HEIF (iOS photos)

Maximum file size: 100MB

After extraction, you can export your data in multiple formats:

  • JSON: For developers and system integration
  • CSV: For spreadsheet applications (Excel, Google Sheets)
  • TXT: Plain text format
  • Excel: Formatted spreadsheet (Pro plans)
  • PDF: Formatted report (Business plans)

For best results, ensure your receipt images:

  • Are well-lit and in focus
  • Show the entire receipt without cropping
  • Are not too small (minimum 640x480 pixels recommended)
  • Have good contrast between text and background

Try different OCR models if one doesn't work well. Florence-2 AI often works best for complex or poor-quality images.
